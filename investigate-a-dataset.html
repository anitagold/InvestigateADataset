Introduction¶

I have chosen to examine the TMDb Movie Dataset from Kaggle. (https://www.kaggle.com/tmdb/tmdb-movie-metadata)

This dataset contains information about appr. 10 thousand movies. It consists of 21 columns such as id, imdb_id, title, popularity, budget, revenue, cast, director, production companies, ratings, homepage, genres, release_date, and average vote.

I decided to ask questions about financials (budget, revenue, and profit) and feedback (popularity and average vote).

Questions asked:¶

  1. Which five movies had the most and the least profit?
  2. How has the average budget, revenue and profit of the movies changed over the years?
  3. What is the correlation between the columns?
  4. Does the budget correlate with the revenue?
  5. Or does the budget correlate with the popularity?
  6. Does the revenue correlate with the average vote?

Answer:¶

  • A correlation matrix is square, with the same variables shown in the rows and columns. The line of 1-s going from the top left to the bottom right is the main diagonal, which shows that each variable always perfectly correlates with itself. This matrix is symmetrical, with the same correlation is shown above the main diagonal being a mirror image of those below the main diagonal.
  • White cell or 0 value means they are not correlated. (like id and vote_average: Correlation coefficient: -0.0013)
  • Red means they are positively correlated (like every columns to itself and profit with the revenue. Correlation coefficient: 0.98)
  • And blue means they are negatively correlated. We can't see strong negative correlation here. There are only lightblues like runtime with release_year which is so weak negative correlation that close to independent. (Correlation coefficient: -0.11)
